Workflow Steps
Quick Reference
Step 1 — Reviewing The RFQ
Before anything else, verify the RFQ details are complete and correct.
✅ What to do
  • Check if the PN and Description are matching
  • Check the QTY of the PN
  • Check the ALT if it is given
  • Check the Comments
Step 2 — Creating The Template
Build a clean email draft before sending any RFQ.
✅ What to do
  • Create a template for each PN
  • Check the quantity (QTY)
  • Check the description
  • Check the Subject PN
  • Check the Font & Size
  • Remove Spaces
🖱️ Where to click
1
Open your email client
2
Create a new email draft for each PN
3
Use the Email Templates page in this Docs panel
Step 3 — Search The PNs
Find stock across all platforms before reaching out.
✅ What to do
  • Search the PN on Google Sheets (to ensure if you worked it or not)
  • Search on Main Platforms: ILS, PARTBASE, LOCATORY, B2B, STOCKMARKET
  • Search on Other Platforms: Satair, AJW, Proponent
  • Ask Manufacturer (Use ChatGPT to look for email)
  • Search Google
  • Ask Co-workers / Check the invoice products
🖱️ Where to click
1
Go to Google Sheets → Internal tracker
2
Open ILS / Partbase / Locatory / B2B / Stockmarket
3
Use the Search & Reminders tab to find emails
Step 4 — Sending The RFQs
Send out requests carefully — no duplicates, no missing info.
✅ What to do
  • Include their name in the email
  • Use the Direct Contact and CC: the General Sales email
  • Check for duplicates before sending (Search @domain.com)
  • Include the QTY in the email
  • Edit the subject header: Single RFQ: "RFQ PN" / Multiple: "RFQ PNs"
  • Be aware: PartBase clients may post offers — do NOT send them a quote
🖱️ Where to click
1
Open your email client → Compose new email
2
Search the Contact Search tab first to find direct emails
3
CC the general sales email (e.g. sales@jadaero.com)
📨 RFQ Template
Subject: RFQ PN [0123FA2] Hello, Please provide your best price quote and stock availability for the following item: 1 PN# [0123FA2] / [SENSOR - LOW FLOW] Thank you. Best regards
⚠ Important Notes
  • BEWARE of Duplicates — always search @domain.com before sending
  • Always include the QTY — suppliers will not quote without it
  • If the RFQ client is from PartBase, they may post offers — do NOT send them a quote
  • Do NOT forget to CC the General Sales email on every RFQ
  • Always address the email with the contact's name — never send a generic "Dear Sir"
Step 5 — Quote Treatments
When a supplier responds, validate every field carefully.
✅ What to check
  • Is the PN matching the PN in your RFQ?
  • Is the description matching?
  • What condition do they have?
  • How many QTY do they have?
  • Is it Stock or with Lead Time?
  • Who is the Tag Src (Supplier)?
  • When was the PN repaired?
  • Is the Quote Outright or Exchange?
🖱️ Where to click
1
Review the email received from the supplier
2
Check the quote against your original RFQ
3
Add every quote to the database — even bad ones
⚠ Important Notes
  • Lead Time means NO certification until the part is repaired — inform the client
  • Add every quote to the database even if it is not good
  • Do NOT accept an Exchange offer without manager approval
  • Do NOT ignore condition discrepancies — OH, AR, SV are not interchangeable without client consent
Step 6 — Certification Review
Check every field of the cert document before proceeding.
✅ What to check
  • Is the PN on the certification matching the PN on the quote?
  • Is the Description on the certification matching?
  • Is the SN on the certification matching?
  • Is the Condition matching?
  • Check Block 12 & 5 (ensure no supplier name is included)
  • Check Block 14a/14b (FAA 8130) and Block 13a (EASA FORM-1)
  • Ensure the date format is MM/DD/YYYY
  • What is the date of repair?
  • Ensure no Signs or Marks used by suppliers are present
🖱️ Where to click
1
Open the certification document received
2
Compare it line-by-line against the supplier quote
3
Use Google to verify date format if unsure
⚠ Important Notes
  • Remove all unwanted paperwork before submitting
  • Review the trace documents for more certainty
  • BEWARE of dates — format is MM/DD/YYYY. Verify the month, year, and day
  • Do NOT submit a certification with a supplier name visible in Block 12 or 5
  • Always double-check Block 14a/14b (FAA) or Block 13a (EASA) before sending

✅ What to do
  • Filter only the suppliers who did NOT quote you
  • Reply with a reminder after 3 hours
  • Include the Sales email and their name/email in the reminder

Step 8 — Picking The Offer
Compare all quotes and select the best one based on correct priority.
✅ What to do
  • Pick the newest Tag Date and preferred Condition
  • Check availability (Stock vs Lead Time)
  • Check the lowest price
  • Search for the Market Price (compare vs your offer)
🖱️ Where to click
1

2

⚠ Important Notes
  • Priority order is: Tag Date › Condition › Price — never compromise Tag Date for price
  • Do NOT pick an offer without confirming availability with the supplier
  • Do NOT choose Exchange without explicit manager approval
Step 9 — The Invoice Process
Fill the invoice from the CERTIFICATION — never from the quote.
✅ What to do
  • Fill the invoice from the CERTIFICATION (not from the quote)
  • Verify: Price, Description, SN, Tag Type, Tag Src, Tag Date, Condition
  • Double-check the Tag Date format (MM/DD/YYYY)
  • CC: Houssam and Moe
  • Set the invoice status to "Initial Email"

Platform Tips & Tricks
How to get the best results from each sourcing platform.
🌐 Stockmarket.com
  • Use exact PN — avoid spaces or dashes
  • Filter by condition: New / OH / AR
  • Check "Last Updated" date on listings
  • Cross-check seller reputation before RFQ
  • Multiple results? Sort by price first
🔧 Partbase
  • Try searching with and without dashes
  • Use "Alternate PNs" section if no results
  • Check cage code to verify manufacturer
  • Request cert docs before confirming offer
🏭 B2B / Locatory
  • Locatory is strong for European stock
  • B2B best for US-based surplus stock
  • Always verify EXW location before quoting
  • Check if listing is from broker or OEM direct
✈️ AJW / Satair
  • Satair = Airbus parts primarily
  • AJW has strong rotable inventory
  • Lead times can be long — always ask
  • Both require account login for full pricing
🔎 Google Search Tips
  • Try: "PN" + "aviation" + "supplier"
  • Try: "PN" + "for sale" + "aircraft"
  • Add site:.com or site:.aero to narrow
  • Use quotes for exact PN match: "12345-6"
  • Check page 2 & 3 — niche suppliers rank low
📋 General Platform Rules
  • Never send RFQ to blacklisted domains
  • Note: QTY / condition / price / lead time
  • One RFQ per supplier per part
  • Screenshot the listing if price changes later
Workflow Tips
Best practices for efficient, accurate sourcing.
✅ Before You Start
  • Confirm QTY and condition with customer first
  • Check if PN has alternates or supersessions
  • Verify PN not already sourced by a colleague
  • Check internal Google Sheet before going external
📧 RFQ Best Practices
  • Always include: PN, QTY, condition required
  • Mention target price if customer gave one
  • Set a reply deadline (usually 24–48h)
  • CC direct contact if available
  • Never forward customer info to supplier
💬 Follow-Up Rules
  • Default follow-up: 3 hours after sending
  • Customer follow-up: 48 hours max
  • Always log result — even if no stock found
  • If no reply after 2 follow-ups, escalate
🚫 Common Mistakes
  • Sending duplicate RFQs to same supplier
  • Quoting without confirming availability
  • Forgetting to note EXW location on multi-offers
  • Acting without manager approval on exchanges
📦 EXW Rules
  • Always note the EXW city and country
  • Multiple EXW → pick closest to buyer
  • Confirm with logistics before promising delivery
  • EXW ≠ DDP — clarify Incoterms with customer
🔄 Re-quoting Tips
  • Always re-quote if a cheaper offer arrives
  • Mention "better offer received" — don't reveal price
  • Get written confirmation before pulling old quote
  • Never re-quote without customer's knowledge

Glossary
Abbreviations and terminology used in aviation parts sourcing.
PN
Part Number — the unique identifier for an aircraft component
RFQ
Request for Quotation — a formal request sent to suppliers asking for price and availability
OEM
Original Equipment Manufacturer — the company that originally made the part
EXW
Ex Works — the part is available at the supplier's location; buyer arranges pickup/shipping
DDP
Delivered Duty Paid — supplier delivers to buyer's door, all costs included
OH
Overhauled — part has been fully inspected, repaired, and certified
AR
As Removed — part taken off an aircraft, condition not tested or certified
SV
Serviceable — part has been inspected and cleared for use
NE / NEW
New — brand new part, never installed
8130
FAA Form 8130-3 — US airworthiness approval tag for aviation parts
EASA F1
EASA Form 1 — European equivalent of the FAA 8130 certification document
PO
Purchase Order — official document confirming an order placed with a supplier
TAT
Turn Around Time — how long a repair or overhaul will take
AOG
Aircraft on Ground — urgent situation; aircraft cannot fly until part is received
BER
Beyond Economical Repair — part is too damaged to repair cost-effectively
CC
Carbon Copy — used in emails to copy additional recipients
Cage Code
5-character code identifying a manufacturer or supplier in the NATO supply system
Incoterms
International trade terms defining who pays for shipping, insurance, and customs
