📚 ECP Workflow Documentation

Welcome to the comprehensive guide for the ECP procurement workflow. This documentation covers all steps from initial RFQ creation through final delivery and certification.

Quick Navigation

🔍 For Buyers

Learn how to request quotes, follow up with suppliers, and manage purchase orders efficiently.

💼 For Sellers

Understand how to send quotations and follow up with potential clients to close deals.

Note: All workflow steps have been optimized for efficiency. Steps are merged where appropriate, and certification is optional based on your needs.

Workflow Overview

The procurement process follows these main stages:

  1. Step 1: Create and send RFQ to suppliers
  2. Step 2: Receive quotes and check availability
  3. Step 3: Follow up if needed (optional)
  4. Step 4: Select best offer and issue PO
  5. Step 5: Process payment and arrange shipping
  6. Certification: Handle certification documents (if applicable)

Step 1: Create RFQ

The Request for Quotation (RFQ) is the first formal step in the procurement process. A well-structured RFQ ensures you receive accurate quotes from suppliers.

Template Examples

Subject: RFQ PN [0123FA2] Hello [Name], Please provide your best price quote and stock availability for the following item: 1 PN# [0123FA2] / [SENSOR - LOW FLOW] Thank you. Best regards
Important: Always replace placeholder text [like this] with actual part numbers, descriptions, and contact names.

Key Information to Include

  • Clear subject line with part number(s)
  • Supplier contact name (personalize when possible)
  • Complete part number and description
  • Required quantity for each item
  • Professional greeting and closing

Step 2: Quote & Availability Check

This step combines receiving quotations and verifying stock availability. Work efficiently by managing both aspects together.

What to Do

📥 Receive Quotes

Collect all quotations from suppliers. Review pricing, lead times, and conditions carefully.

✅ Verify Availability

Confirm stock status with suppliers. Check if items are in stock or require manufacturing time.

Evaluation Checklist

  • Price: Compare unit prices and total costs
  • Availability: Confirm stock status and lead time
  • Conditions: Review warranty, certification, and return policies
  • Shipping: Check shipping terms (FOB, EXW, etc.)
  • Payment: Understand payment terms and methods
Pro Tip: Create a comparison spreadsheet to evaluate multiple quotes side-by-side for easier decision making.

Step 3: Follow-Up (if needed)

If you haven't received a response to your RFQ, send a polite follow-up reminder to suppliers.

Follow-Up Templates

Hello, Just a quick kind reminder regarding our RFQ. Best regards,

Follow-Up Best Practices

  • Wait 2-3 business days before sending first follow-up
  • Keep the message brief and professional
  • Reference the original RFQ date if applicable
  • Avoid being overly aggressive or demanding
  • Consider calling if email doesn't get a response
Note: Some suppliers receive hundreds of RFQs daily. A polite reminder can help your request stand out.

Step 4: Best Offer Selection & Purchase Order

This step combines selecting the best offer and issuing the purchase order. Streamline your procurement by handling both together.

Process Overview

🏆 Select Best Offer

Compare all received quotes. Consider price, availability, quality, and supplier reliability.

📄 Issue Purchase Order

Once selected, immediately generate and send the PO to the chosen supplier to secure the order.

Selection Criteria

  • Total Cost: Include price, shipping, taxes, and fees
  • Delivery Time: Ensure lead time meets your requirements
  • Supplier Reputation: Consider past performance and reviews
  • Payment Terms: Evaluate flexibility and security
  • Documentation: Confirm required certifications are included

PO Must Include

  • Complete supplier and buyer contact information
  • Detailed item description with part numbers
  • Agreed unit price and total amount
  • Shipping address and method
  • Payment terms and due dates
  • Expected delivery date
  • PO number for tracking
Important: Always get written confirmation from the supplier that they accept your PO terms before considering the order final.

Step 5: Payment Processing & Shipping Arrangement

Handle payment and coordinate shipping logistics to ensure smooth delivery of your order.

Workflow

💳 Process Payment

Execute payment according to agreed terms. Keep all transaction records for reference.

🚚 Arrange Shipping

Coordinate with supplier on shipping method, carrier, and tracking information.

Payment Methods

  • Wire Transfer: Most common for international transactions
  • Credit Card: Offers buyer protection but may have fees
  • PayPal/Online: Quick and convenient for smaller amounts
  • Letter of Credit: Secure option for large international orders
  • Net Terms: Payment after delivery (for established relationships)

Shipping Coordination

  • Confirm shipping method (air, sea, ground)
  • Get tracking number and estimated delivery date
  • Ensure proper packaging for the items
  • Verify insurance coverage if needed
  • Coordinate customs clearance for international shipments
Pro Tip: Request a commercial invoice and packing list before shipment to prepare for customs if applicable.

Certification Documentation (Optional)

Certification is only required for certain aerospace, aviation, or regulated industry parts. Click below to view certification requirements if applicable to your order.

When Certification is Required

Certification documents are necessary when:

  • Parts are for aviation/aerospace use
  • Components are safety-critical or regulated
  • Customer specifically requests certified materials
  • Industry standards (FAA, EASA, etc.) mandate traceability

Common Certification Types

FAA Form 8130-3

Authorized Release Certificate for US aviation parts. Required for FAA-regulated aircraft maintenance.

EASA Form 1

European equivalent of FAA 8130-3. Required for EASA-regulated operations in Europe.

Certificate of Conformance

Manufacturer's statement that parts meet specified requirements and standards.

Material Test Reports

Lab test results showing material composition and properties meet specifications.

Verification Steps

  1. Request Early: Ask for certification details during quoting phase
  2. Verify Authenticity: Check certification against issuing authority databases
  3. Review Details: Ensure part numbers, serial numbers, and specs match exactly
  4. Store Safely: Keep digital and physical copies of all certifications
  5. Track Expiry: Some certifications have validity periods - monitor expiration dates
Critical: Never accept parts requiring certification without proper documentation. Using uncertified parts in regulated industries can result in serious legal and safety consequences.

What to Check on Certificates

  • Issuing authority name and signature
  • Part number matches your order
  • Serial number (if serialized part)
  • Condition code (new, overhauled, repaired, etc.)
  • Date of certification/inspection
  • Authorized inspector or company stamp
  • Traceability information to manufacturer
Note: If certification is not required for your order, you can safely skip this section. Not all parts need formal certification.

Sending Quotation to Clients

When responding to customer RFQs, use these professional quotation templates.

Quotation Template

Subject: QUOTE FOR PN: [PN#] FROM JAD AEROSPACE LLC Hello [Client Name], Following your RFQ please find below our quote: PN: [PN#] / Description: [DESC] / QTY: [X] each SN: [SN#] Cond: RP Tag Type: MFG 8130/CofC/ATA106/DUAL FAA/EASA FORM-1 Tag Src: General MRO Aerospace Inc. Tag Date: [MM/DD/YYYY] Warranty: 6 MONTHS EXW: USA Lead Time: Stock Asking Price: $[PRICE] Validity of quotation: 7 days or subject to prior sale. We look forward to receiving your feedback. Thank you for giving us the opportunity to send you this quote. Kind regards.

Quote Best Practices

  • Respond to RFQs within 24-48 hours when possible
  • Include all relevant technical details and certifications
  • Be clear about pricing validity period
  • Specify exact lead times and stock status
  • Include warranty information
  • State shipping terms clearly (EXW, FOB, CIF, etc.)

Following Up on Sent Quotations

If a client hasn't responded to your quote, use these follow-up templates to re-engage them professionally.

Follow-Up Templates

Hello, Just following up on this quote. Please advise if you have received a better offer so we can work to get as close as possible. Looking forward to your feedback. Thank you! Best Regards

Follow-Up Strategy

  • First Follow-Up: 3-4 days after sending quote
  • Second Follow-Up: 1 week after first follow-up
  • Final Follow-Up: 2 weeks after second (then move on)
  • Always offer to match or beat competitor pricing
  • Show willingness to negotiate and work with the client
  • Keep tone professional and helpful, never pushy
Pro Tip: In your follow-up, ask if they received a better offer. This opens the door for negotiation and shows you're flexible on pricing.
