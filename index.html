Tips & Tricks per Platform
🌐 Stockmarket.com
  • Use exact PN — avoid spaces or dashes
  • Filter by condition: New / OH / AR
  • Check "Last Updated" date on listings
  • Cross-check seller reputation before RFQ
  • Multiple results? Sort by price first
🔧 Partbase
  • Try searching with and without dashes
  • Use "Alternate PNs" section if no results
  • Check cage code to verify manufacturer
  • Request cert docs before confirming offer
🏭 B2B / Locatory
  • Locatory is strong for European stock
  • B2B best for US-based surplus stock
  • Always verify EXW location before quoting
  • Check if listing is from broker or OEM direct
✈️ AJW / Satair
  • Satair = Airbus parts primarily
  • AJW has strong rotable inventory
  • Lead times on these can be long — always ask
  • Both require account login for full pricing
🔎 Google Search Tips
  • Try: "PN" + "aviation" + "supplier"
  • Try: "PN" + "for sale" + "aircraft"
  • Add site:.com or site:.aero to narrow results
  • Use quotes for exact PN match: "12345-6"
  • Check page 2 & 3 — niche suppliers rank low
📋 General Platform Rules
  • Never send RFQ to blacklisted domains
  • Always note: QTY / condition / price / lead time
  • One RFQ per supplier per part — no duplicates
  • Screenshot the listing if price changes later
General Workflow Tips
✅ Before You Start
  • Confirm QTY and condition with customer first
  • Check if PN has alternates or supersessions
  • Verify the PN is not already being sourced by a colleague
  • Check internal Google Sheet before going external
📧 RFQ Best Practices
  • Always include: PN, QTY, condition required
  • Mention target price if customer gave one
  • Set a reply deadline (usually 24–48h)
  • CC direct contact if available
  • Never forward customer info to supplier
💬 Follow-Up Rules
  • Default follow-up: 3 hours after sending
  • Customer follow-up: 48 hours max
  • Always log result — even if no stock found
  • If no reply after 2 follow-ups, escalate
🚫 Common Mistakes
  • Sending duplicate RFQs to same supplier
  • Quoting without confirming availability
  • Forgetting to note EXW location on multi-offers
  • Acting without manager approval on exchanges
  • Using old or unverified contact emails
📦 EXW Rules
  • Always note the EXW city and country
  • Multiple EXW offers → pick closest to buyer
  • Confirm with logistics before promising delivery
  • EXW ≠ DDP — clarify Incoterms with customer
🔄 Re-quoting Tips
  • Always re-quote if a cheaper offer arrives
  • Mention "better offer received" — don't reveal price
  • Get written confirmation before pulling old quote
  • Never re-quote without customer's knowledge

Abbreviations & Terminology Glossary
PN
Part Number — the unique identifier for an aircraft component
RFQ
Request for Quotation — a formal request sent to suppliers asking for price and availability
OEM
Original Equipment Manufacturer — the company that originally made the part
EXW
Ex Works — the part is available at the supplier's location; buyer arranges pickup/shipping
DDP
Delivered Duty Paid — supplier delivers to buyer's door, all costs included
OH
Overhauled — part has been fully inspected, repaired, and certified
AR
As Removed — part taken off an aircraft, condition not tested or certified
SV
Serviceable — part has been inspected and cleared for use
NE / NEW
New — brand new part, never installed
8130
FAA Form 8130-3 — US airworthiness approval tag for aviation parts
EASA F1
EASA Form 1 — European equivalent of the FAA 8130 certification document
PO
Purchase Order — official document confirming an order placed with a supplier
TAT
Turn Around Time — how long a repair or overhaul will take
AOG
Aircraft on Ground — urgent situation; aircraft cannot fly until part is received
BER
Beyond Economical Repair — part is too damaged to repair cost-effectively
CC
Carbon Copy — used in emails to copy additional recipients (e.g. direct contacts)
Cage Code
5-character code identifying a manufacturer or supplier in the NATO supply system
Incoterms
International trade terms defining who pays for shipping, insurance, and customs
